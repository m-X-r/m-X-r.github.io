The Moon

While I was learning to code I zero-ed in on HTML. Mostly because I could do it on any computer (I work on a lot of shared computers). I didn't have to download any code readers and I could see my results immediatly. One day I was at my local library and I saw a childrens book on display.

How to be on the Moon Vivian Schwarz

I thought it was a cute story,so I decided to try and make it into a game with HTML. Before I did though I wanted to know what other books Vivian had written and I found one with the same main character. Its called How to find Gold. It was also cute, so I wanted to add it into my game idea as well.

The thought process I have now has the game starting off with an online dating app. You try to find your dates house and get lost. The only person you find to help you says you have to help her take a trip to the moon before she helps you. And of course you cannot get to the moon without finding gold first. So if you want to go on that date (you really, really do) you have to find gold and get to the moon first. Better not be late!

Sadly, I haven't finished the game yet. When I do finish it I will put a link to it HERE

Monsters

So I like monsters I guess. I draw them and make cologes of them, sometimes I even make paper toys of them. I'm not the best draw-er, so sometimes they look kinda boxy (expecially if I make a paper toy of them).

Because of their sometimes boxy deminor, some people (not you of course) confuse them with robots. I dont like that, they dont like it either, but there is nothing I can do about it. What I could do is make people more aware of monsters. They just dont get their due in my opinion. You are scarred of the monster in your closet or under your bed when you are small. But by the time you grow up you have forgotten about them. They grew up too you know. You should check in on them. To help I started a Blog about monsters and their comings and goings. Like a newsletter full of employee of the months, but instead its monsters.
